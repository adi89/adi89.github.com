The Resume

It's pretty interesting. I only graduated a year ago. I earned a Biomedical Engineering degree, something I had chased since high school, but in senior year I had my mind set on something else. I want to build things. As crass as it sounds, I want to put my name on something and have the satifcation of having gotten out what I put in. So I knew what I had to do. I put my nose to the grindstone and got to work. After school, I worked as an Inspector at the Battery Maritime Building post-Sandy. It was a hard time having to manage myself and learn the intricacies of the counterweight drawbridge system. I did so with the image of pursuing Web Development in the forefront of my mind. I showed up a fish out of water at Web Dev Immersive, but I've really made strides at improving. I've came a long ways in short while, but I've just gotten started. Eargasm is an app I presented in front of the first NYC rails meetup at GA. Think of a friend circle that recommends each other music with information from Echnoest for music info and SongKickr for concert recommondations geocoded at your fingertips. That's Eargasm.

The Gos is the ultimate homage to Ryan Gosling. Summoning the power of Twilio, you can send your own "Hey Girl" texts, and even receive a message courtesy of the man himself. Additionally, you can keep tabs on Ryan with the updating newsfeed or watch clips at your leisure via the youtube api.

Mellowgasm is a vision I had since before this class: the ability to navigate across nightlife based on WHO enters. We used the foursquare API to track down images of a given venue's frequent visitors. Did I mention this isn't exclusive to manhattan? Yeah, we also have an algorithm that factors in foursquare rating, checkins, and the girl-guy ratio.
